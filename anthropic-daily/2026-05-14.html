Two partnerships, different scales entirely: PwC is putting Claude Code in front of 30,000 professionals; the Gates Foundation is putting $200M toward the 4.6 billion who have no access to essential health services. Claude Code v2.1.142 ships the same day with Fast mode upgraded to Opus 4.7.

Lead

Anthropic and the Gates Foundation committed $200 million on May 13 to put Claude into global health, life sciences, education, and agricultural productivity programs in low- and middle-income countries. The $200M covers grant funding, Claude usage credits, and technical support over four years. Health gets the largest allocation: accelerating vaccine and therapy development, helping governments act on population health data, and researching diseases that attract no commercial investment because the patients can't pay. Polio, HPV, and eclampsia/preeclampsia are the opening targets. (Gates Foundation press release; Anthropic announcement, May 13.)

Around 4.6 billion people worldwide lack access to essential health services, per the Foundation's own framing. Education targets K-12 tutoring in the U.S. and foundational literacy and numeracy programs in sub-Saharan Africa and India. Agriculture focuses on smallholder farming productivity for nearly two billion people whose incomes depend on it.

The structure mirrors the enterprise push: Anthropic provides the reasoning layer, sector partners carry the integration surface, and the Gates Foundation brings three decades of on-the-ground infrastructure in exactly the places Claude will need it.

PwC and Anthropic expanded their alliance to put Claude Code and Claude Cowork in front of PwC's U.S. workforce, with 30,000 professionals being trained on Anthropic models. Rollout begins U.S.-first, then extends toward PwC's 364,000-person global workforce in 136 countries. (PwC press release)

Claude Code v2.1.142

Fast mode now defaults to Opus 4.7 (was Opus 4.6). New claude agents flags for configuring dispatched background sessions: --add-dir, --settings, --mcp-config, --plugin-dir, --permission-mode, --model, --effort, --dangerously-skip-permissions. Plugins with a root-level SKILL.md and no skills/ subdirectory are now automatically surfaced as a skill. The /plugin details pane and claude plugin details now show LSP servers a plugin provides. /web-setup warns before replacing an existing GitHub App connection.

Bug fixes: MCP_TOOL_TIMEOUT now correctly raises the per-request fetch timeout for remote HTTP and SSE MCP servers (previously capped tool calls at 60 seconds regardless of configured value). Background sessions not recognizing pre-existing git worktrees fixed. macOS sleep/wake daemon reconnect fixed. Daemon exit on binary upgrade fixed.

How to use: claude update or reinstall. Set CLAUDE_CODE_OPUS_4_6_FAST_MODE_OVERRIDE=1 to pin fast mode back to Opus 4.6.
