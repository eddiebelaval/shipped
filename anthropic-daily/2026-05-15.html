Two institutional bets landed in the same 24 hours: one for humanity's overlooked diseases, one for a Big Four firm's headcount.

Lead

The Gates Foundation committed $200 million in grant funding, Claude usage credits, and technical support to work with Anthropic on global health, education, economic mobility, and language accessibility over four years. The starting point is specific: polio, HPV, and eclampsia. Not abstract “healthcare.” Claude is already screening drug candidates and catching patterns in systematic reviews (Anthropic, Gates Foundation); the partnership formalizes and funds that work at scale.

The education arm runs two tracks: K-12 tutoring in the US, foundational literacy and numeracy in sub-Saharan Africa and India. The language component addresses a structural gap AI systems have ignored for years. Dozens of African languages have inadequate training data. The partnership will fund collection and labeling and release it publicly, which means the upside goes beyond Claude.
