Apple opened WWDC this morning with iOS 27 Extensions: users will choose Claude as their AI model for Siri. The iPhone gains a Claude option for the first time.

Lead Story

WWDC 2026 opened Monday at Apple's Cupertino campus with the announcement that defined the morning: iOS 27 introduces an Extensions framework, and Claude is in the first-party lineup. Users who install Claude's iOS app can designate it as their preferred AI engine for Siri queries, Writing Tools, and Image Playground. When Claude handles a Siri request, the response comes in a distinct voice so users know which model answered.

The arrangement is not equal. Google's Gemini holds the native integration slot backed by a reported $1 billion annual agreement with Apple. Claude and ChatGPT enter as user-installed providers layered on top of that foundation. Gemini runs the house. Claude comes in through the front door, but Gemini cut its own key.

iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 ship fall 2026. Reported by 9to5Mac and confirmed at WWDC per TechTimes. Anthropic has not issued a press release as of sweep time.
