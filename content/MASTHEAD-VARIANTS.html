My take

A — Period orange (current): Direct. Simple. The period IS the brand thesis. Easy to recognize, easy to render anywhere, scales to any size. The most defensible.

B — Tittle orange, period stays ink: The most sophisticated. The orange becomes an inside-the-wordmark craft move instead of punctuation. Rewards careful looking. The tittle survives even when the period gets stripped (favicons, app icons, small contexts). My favorite of the four.

C — Both orange: Too much. The eye bounces between two orange dots and neither lands. Skip.

D — Tittle only, period dropped: A real rebrand. Loses the "done, final, shipped" thesis the period carried. Could work but needs you to commit to a new brand premise.

Recommendation: Variant B. Orange tittle on the 'i', period stays ink. You keep the period as quiet structural punctuation (the brand still says "Shipped." with the period intact), but the orange becomes a craft moment readers discover inside the wordmark. Most magazine-grade move of the four. FedEx arrow energy.

Note: at production scale we'd render the masthead as SVG (the way Iris's masthead.svg already does it) so the tittle dot is mathematically perfect and survives subpixel rendering. The CSS approach above is the demo.
