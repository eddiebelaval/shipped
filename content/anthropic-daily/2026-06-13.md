## Lead

### The directive

Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick's letter reached Dario Amodei at 5:21 PM ET on Thursday, June 12. The order: suspend all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for any foreign national, inside or outside the United States, including Anthropic's own foreign-national employees. Anthropic took both models offline for every customer worldwide.

The trigger, per Axios: a claim from another company that it had jailbroken Mythos, alarming the administration about national security risks. The Commerce Department's position was that a technique existed to bypass Fable 5's safety classifiers, which gate access to Mythos's cybersecurity capabilities. Anthropic disputes the characterization and is working to restore access. The department has not disclosed specifics.

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 launched June 9. That is 72 hours from general availability to global suspension. The directive arrived with no advance notice to Anthropic, and its scope (all foreign nationals, anywhere, including employees) was broad enough to force Anthropic to disable both models for every customer rather than attempt targeted enforcement.
