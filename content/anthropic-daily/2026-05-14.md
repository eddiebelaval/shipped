**LEAD**

Anthropic and the Gates Foundation committed $200 million on May 13 to put Claude into global health, life sciences, education, and agricultural productivity programs in low- and middle-income countries. The $200M covers grant funding, Claude usage credits, and technical support over four years. Health gets the largest allocation: accelerating vaccine and therapy development, helping governments act on population health data, and researching diseases that attract no commercial investment because the patients can't pay. Polio, HPV, and eclampsia/preeclampsia are the opening targets. (Gates Foundation press release, https://www.gatesfoundation.org/ideas/media-center/press-releases/2026/05/ai-anthropic-partnership; Anthropic, https://www.anthropic.com/news/gates-foundation-partnership; reported Reuters, Yahoo Finance, May 13.)

Around 4.6 billion people worldwide lack access to essential health services, per the Foundation's own framing. Education targets K-12 tutoring in the U.S. and foundational literacy and numeracy programs in sub-Saharan Africa and India. Agriculture focuses on smallholder farming productivity for nearly two billion people whose incomes depend on it.

The structure mirrors the enterprise push: Anthropic provides the reasoning layer, sector partners carry the integration surface, the Gates Foundation brings three decades of on-the-ground infrastructure in exactly the places Claude will need it.
