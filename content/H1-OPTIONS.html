How to choose

01 · Fraunces — modern, expressive italics, variable optical-size axis means one face carries 256px display AND 19px body. Most flexible. Currently in temp-100.

02 · Libre Baskerville — classical and restrained. Says "credibility, not drama." Iris picked this for the lab-coat fit.

03 · Playfair Display — Vogue energy. Strong vertical contrast, sharp serifs. Reads as "magazine" instantly. Maybe too fashion-magazine for tech?

04 · Bodoni Moda — extreme contrast Didone. Maximum drama. Harper's Bazaar register. Beautiful but uncompromising — body usability suffers at small sizes.

05 · DM Serif Display — clean modern serif, display-only. Body would have to be Inter. The most "magazine-website" of the lot — feels like *The Atlantic* online.

06 · Newsreader — designed for editorial reading on screen. Quiet authority. NYT-adjacent. Less drama, more substance.

My read: If you want maximum drama and brand presence, 04 (Bodoni Moda). If you want flexibility and one-face-does-everything, 01 (Fraunces). If you want quiet credible authority, 06 (Newsreader). Tell me which one lands and I'll lock it in across DESIGN.md and rebuild MOCKUP-FINAL.html.
