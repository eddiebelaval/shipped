# Shipped., Week of 2026-06-01 (Anthropic)

**Cover Line:** The IPO filing landed Monday. By Tuesday, Anthropic had set a public timeline for the model it once called too dangerous to release.

---

## The Open

Monday, June 1. Anthropic submitted a confidential draft registration statement on Form S-1 to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission. A brief post on anthropic.com. No live stream, no coordinated media moment. ([anthropic.com](https://www.anthropic.com/news/confidential-draft-s1-sec)) The direction: public markets. The timeline: as early as October 2026.

Thirty-four hours later, Anthropic expanded Project Glasswing to 150 new organizations across more than 15 countries. Power grids. Water systems. Healthcare providers. Communications companies. Hardware manufacturers. Samsung and NATO confirmed as additions, per Financial Times reporting cited by SiliconANGLE. ([anthropic.com](https://www.anthropic.com/news/expanding-project-glasswing), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/anthropic-mythos-ai-project-glasswing.html)) Buried near the bottom of the announcement: Mythos-class models will be available to all customers "in the coming weeks," once additional safeguards are finalized.

Two days. An IPO filing and a public timeline for the model the company had classified as too dangerous for general release. These are not separate narratives. They are the same bet, announced 34 hours apart.

---

## The Lead Story

The S-1 mechanics are these. Anthropic submitted a confidential draft on Form S-1 to the SEC on June 1, 2026. Confidential status means the document's risk factors, revenue details, margins, and growth trajectory stay private until the company elects to disclose them. What is public: the direction and the scale. ([anthropic.com](https://www.anthropic.com/news/confidential-draft-s1-sec))

What had already leaked into reporting: the annualized revenue run rate has crossed $47 billion. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/01/anthropic-ipo-s1-prospectus.html), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/01/nx-s1-5843199/anthropic-ipo-filing-ai-large)) That number deserves a frame. Salesforce, which built its enterprise platform over two decades, reported $34.9 billion for all of fiscal 2024. Anthropic's run rate is 35 percent above that. The velocity: $9 billion at year-end 2025, $14 billion in February, $30 billion in April, $47 billion now. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/29/anthropic-raises-65-billion-at-record-965-billion-valuation-promises-mythos-ai-model-in-wide-release-in-coming-weeks-releases-claude-opus-4-8/)) Four data points in five months. The line is nearly vertical.

Target listing: as early as October 2026. Wilson Sonsini, the firm that handled Google's 2004 IPO, is reportedly engaged as legal counsel. No share count. No price range. Both wait on market conditions and the eventual public prospectus.

What the filing will need to address: the cost of safety. Anthropic's Responsible Scaling Policy, alignment research compute, the Glasswing infrastructure, and the interpretability evaluations required to clear Mythos for public release are all real expenditures. None appear in the run-rate number. Public markets will want to know how they flow through to margins.

What the filing will not need to justify, at this valuation: the market's belief in the product. $47 billion in annualized revenue settled that argument. The S-1, when it becomes public, is the document that explains the gap between the safety story and the growth story. It will be the read of the year.

## Also Shipped

**Project Glasswing: 150 new organizations.** Anthropic added 150 organizations across 15-plus countries to Project Glasswing, bringing total partners to approximately 200. ([anthropic.com](https://www.anthropic.com/news/expanding-project-glasswing)) New sectors: power, water, healthcare, communications, hardware. Samsung and NATO confirmed. Partners have collectively found more than 10,000 high- or critical-severity security flaws in widely deployed systems since launch, per CNBC. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/anthropic-mythos-ai-project-glasswing.html)) The general release clause embedded in the announcement is the news: Mythos-class models are coming to all customers "in the coming weeks," once additional safeguards are ready. The access program is not ending. It is becoming the on-ramp to a public product.

**Claude Code v2.1.160 (security pass).** Anthropic added explicit prompts before Claude writes to shell startup files (.zshenv, .zlogin, .bash_login) and to ~/.config/git/. In acceptEdits mode, build-tool config files that can grant code execution (.npmrc, .yarnrc, bunfig.toml, .bazelrc, .pre-commit-config.yaml, .devcontainer/) now require explicit approval. A new /config setting, "Workflow keyword trigger," controls whether the word "workflow" in a prompt auto-starts a Dynamic Workflow. ([GitHub](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/releases))

**Claude Code v2.1.161 (observability and credentials).** OTEL_RESOURCE_ATTRIBUTES values now appear as labels on metric datapoints, enabling custom-dimension slicing by team or repo. Agent view rows show done/total progress when work fans out. MCP secrets (credential headers, URL secrets) that were being printed by `claude mcp list/get/add` are now redacted. Parallel tool calls: a failed bash command no longer cancels other calls in the same batch. ([GitHub](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/releases))

**Service disruption (resolved).** Elevated error rates across Claude AI, Claude Console, Claude API, and Claude Code were reported starting at 2:19 AM ET on June 2. Root cause identified and resolved, per SQ Magazine. No public post-mortem at time of this compilation.

## Quiet on the Wire

The API, Claude Apps, Claude Code, and system-prompt release notes documentation pages returned HTTP 403 on both sweep days this week. Python SDK (last: v0.105.2, May 29) and TypeScript SDK (last: v0.100.1, May 29) have not published new releases. ([GitHub Python SDK](https://github.com/anthropics/anthropic-sdk-python/releases)) No research papers confirmed for the June 1-2 window. The operative story this week is policy and corporate structure, not platform.

## The Close

Anthropic filed for its IPO on Monday.

On Tuesday it expanded the program to find vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure and set a public timeline for its most powerful model.

The prospectus, when it arrives, will be the read of the year.
