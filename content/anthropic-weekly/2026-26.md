# Shipped., Week of 2026-06-22 (Anthropic)

**Cover line:** Five versions shipped, Claude went offline for two hours and forty-two minutes, and Anthropic accused Alibaba of fraudulently extracting the capabilities both events put on display.

---

## The Open

Tuesday, 10:02 AM ET. Every Claude model threw errors at the same instant. By 10:13 AM, outage trackers logged 7,119 simultaneous US reports. Eleven minutes of alerts. Two hours and forty-two minutes of downtime. Claude for Government never went down. No postmortem had landed by end of week. ([Source: TechRadar](https://www.techradar.com/news/live/claude-down-june-23-2026))

Two days later, Anthropic sent a formal letter to US senators and White House officials accusing Alibaba's Qwen AI lab of running a coordinated campaign using thousands of fraudulent accounts to extract Claude's capabilities in software engineering and agentic reasoning. ([Source: Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/alibaba-drops-after-anthropic-accuses-firm-of-accessing-ai-model), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/anthropic-alibaba-distillation-campaign.html)) Alibaba ADRs fell to a 16-month low in Hong Kong. Anthropic has not published the letter.

The rest of the week: five Claude Code versions shipped Monday through Friday. A Slack integration went live that puts Claude inside workspaces as a named, persistent team member. The Python and TypeScript SDKs shipped in parallel, both adding system-level streaming events. A research report landed Friday mapping Claude usage to the rhythms of the outside world. In the background, Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remained suspended under a US export control directive that has been in force since June 12, while Fable 5's complimentary access window on paid plans quietly closed June 22 without a formal announcement.

---

## Lead Story: The Letter

On June 24 (CNBC first reported), confirmed and expanded by Bloomberg on June 25, Anthropic formally accused Alibaba's Qwen AI lab of running a large-scale operation to extract Claude's capabilities using thousands of fraudulent accounts. The targets named in the accusation: software engineering performance and agentic reasoning. Alibaba ADRs fell to a 16-month low in Hong Kong trading following the disclosure. Anthropic has not published the letter. Alibaba has not commented.

The choice of venue is the story. IP theft complaints go to courts. Trade disputes with geopolitical weight go to Congress. Anthropic went to Congress. That doesn't make the underlying accusation false, but it tells you Anthropic sees this as a policy argument, not just a legal one.

This week's letter said Alibaba's Qwen lab specifically targeted software engineering and agentic reasoning. Those are not incidental capabilities. They are the commercial core of what Claude does, and they are what Claude Code, which shipped five versions in five days this week, is built on. The week where Alibaba was accused of fraudulently acquiring those capabilities was also the week Anthropic shipped the most rapid public iteration of the product built from them.

The political context matters. Fable 5 and Mythos 5 have been off the API since June 12, suspended following a public jailbreak demonstration and a US government export control directive. Anthropic published a statement disagreeing with the restriction but complying. ([Source: Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access)) The letter to Congress creates a coherent posture: the company that accepted external constraints on its own frontier models while pressing government to constrain those who would extract those models' capabilities through fraud.

Expect regulatory coverage. The story is now in the pipeline of multiple lawmakers' offices.

## Also Shipped

**Claude Tag: Claude as a Named Slack Teammate**
Announced June 23. Anthropic put Claude inside Slack as a persistent, named team member rather than a bot account. Every employee in an enabled workspace works with the same Claude, so task handoffs between teammates preserve context without starting fresh. The ambient mode is the detail worth flagging: Claude can follow up on inactive threads without being explicitly tagged. Research preview for Claude Enterprise and Team users. Runs on Claude Opus 4.8. ([Source: Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-claude-tag))

**SDK Pair Ships system.message Streaming**
June 24. Python SDK v0.112.0 and TypeScript SDK v0.106.0 shipped in parallel. Both add `system.message` streaming events, giving clients access to system-level events mid-conversation alongside the existing `content_block_*` stream. Both also add a new refusal stop reason category and User Profile ID support in request headers. The Python release fixed a memory tool parent directory permissions bug; the TypeScript release fixed `x-stainless-helper` header semantics. ([Python SDK](https://github.com/anthropics/anthropic-sdk-python/releases/tag/v0.112.0), [TypeScript SDK](https://github.com/anthropics/anthropic-sdk-typescript/releases/tag/sdk-v0.106.0))

**Tuesday Outage: Two Hours and Forty-Two Minutes**
June 23, 10:02 AM to 12:44 PM ET. Elevated error rates across all Claude models and platforms. Peak: 7,119 simultaneous US outage reports at 10:13 AM. Claude for Government was unaffected. No cause or postmortem published by end of week. ([Source: TechRadar](https://www.techradar.com/news/live/claude-down-june-23-2026))

## Quiet on the Wire

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain off the API. The US government export control directive, issued June 12 after a jailbreak was publicly demonstrated on X, has not been lifted as of week's end. Anthropic published a statement calling the action a response to a narrow potential jailbreak, disagreeing with it, and complying. Separately, complimentary access to Fable 5 on Pro, Max, Team, and seat-based Enterprise plans ended June 22, reverting to standard plan entitlements. No formal announcement of the transition was published.
