# Shipped., Week of 2026-07-06 (Anthropic)

**Cover line:** On the same Friday OpenAI asked you to hand over the work, Anthropic shipped a product asking whether you should.

---

## The Open

Friday, somewhere between 9 AM and noon. OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work: give us your tasks, get back finished spreadsheets, slide decks, web apps, full documents. Hand the machine your next six hours. Come back when it's done.

Same Friday. Anthropic shipped Reflect, a beta feature inside Claude settings that shows you your monthly topic distribution, your most active day, your peak hour, and the patterns running across every session you've ever had. It asks reflective questions. The example from the announcement: "What's one thing you want to keep doing yourself, even if Claude could do it faster?"

That question, on that day, in that sequence, is not an accident.

The rest of the week was infrastructure. Anthropic shipped five versions of Claude Code, from v2.1.202 on Monday to v2.1.206 on Thursday night. They ran a government case study out of Alberta that covered 466 million lines of provincial code in 20 hours. They expanded Cowork to web and mobile, deepened the Microsoft 365 integration, and launched Claude for Government Desktop in public beta with FedRAMP High authorization. They ended Fable 5's complimentary access on paid plans and moved it to usage credits. And on Friday evening, a new SDK flag showed up without documentation: "dreaming."

One question. Five code versions. A government contract. A quiet flag nobody has explained yet. That is the week.

---

## Lead Story: Reflect and the Hard Question

The Reflect feature is simple to describe. Enable Memory in Claude settings. Open Settings. Find Reflect. It surfaces your usage history as a set of patterns: what topics you bring to Claude most, what hours you reach for it, how that distribution shifts across the month. It adds a reflective prompt above the data. The feature is in beta. It is available on Free, Pro, and Max plans. Incognito chats and health-integration conversations are excluded by design. ([Source: Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news))

That is the product. The positioning is a different story.

The Inviting Hard Questions campaign shipped alongside it. A film, commissioned through agency Mother. A public question submission site at [claude.com/hard-questions](https://claude.com/hard-questions). Research drawn from 52,000 Americans surveyed and 81,000 Claude users across 159 countries and 70 languages. The concerns documented: job loss, devaluing creative work, reduced human agency, and diminished ability to think independently. Anthropic committed publicly to tracking and reporting specific actions it takes in response, including areas where it falls short. ([Source: claude.com/hard-questions](https://claude.com/hard-questions))

That accountability framing, done in public, in an announcement, with a specific commitment to report back on failures, is unusual. Companies that build AI announce capabilities. They announce safeguards. They rarely announce that they surveyed 81,000 of their own users about whether the product is doing harm, found four categories of documented concern, and promised to answer for them.

The timing is precise. When OpenAI launched ChatGPT, Anthropic published Constitutional AI papers. When GPT-4 launched, Anthropic launched Claude 2 and a character card. When ChatGPT Work launched, Anthropic launched Reflect and a hard-questions campaign. You can read this as reactive positioning or as consistent identity. Either read lands in the same place: Anthropic has decided that the distinctive thing it can offer is not a faster agent or a cheaper output tier. It is a company willing to run a 52,000-person survey about whether its product is good for you, and then publish what they found.

Whether that bet pays off commercially is a separate question. The bet is placed. The week made it visible.

## Also Shipped

**Alberta's two-agent scan.** The Government of Alberta's Ministry of Technology and Innovation built a two-agent security review system using Claude Code with Opus and Sonnet models, ran it against the province's entire codebase, and published the result on July 6: 466 million lines scanned, vulnerabilities remediated, new tooling built. Time elapsed: 20 hours. A red team agent probed as an attacker would; a blue team agent wrote remediation plans pointing to exact files. The province reported 20x speed improvement and up to 95% reduction in modernization timelines. An industry day in Edmonton shares the playbook with other governments. Province-wide scale-out begins fall 2026. ([Source: Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/alberta-government-claude-cybersecurity))

This is not a headline about AI potential. It is a headline about a government that shipped.

**Cowork goes infrastructure.** Over July 7 and 8, Anthropic expanded Cowork to web and mobile, with sessions now running in the cloud by default: work continues when the laptop closes or the device switches. Chat and Cowork share one home tab in the Claude interface. The Microsoft 365 integration deepened: draft and send email from inside Claude, manage calendar events, create and update OneDrive and SharePoint files. Entra admin consent required for write operations. Doubled usage limits extended through August 5. Simultaneously, Claude for Government Desktop launched in public beta: FedRAMP High authorized, local conversation history on agency-managed devices, department-level administration, spending and model limits, tamper-evident audit logs. ([Source: Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news))

Taken together: draft-and-send email from inside a Claude session, audit logs for department-level compliance, and FedRAMP High in the same announcement. That is not a chatbot with extensions. That is an enterprise software procurement conversation.

**Fable 5 starts the meter.** Effective July 8, Fable 5's complimentary access on Pro, Max, Team, and select Enterprise plans ended. The model that was included at no extra cost through July 7 now requires usage credits: $10 per million input tokens, $50 per million output tokens. No announcement preceded the change. The same week Anthropic expanded Cowork as the platform that justifies a subscription, it moved the frontier model off the free list. Cowork earns the plan. Fable 5 is the upsell for the teams that need it. ([Source: Anthropic pricing](https://www.anthropic.com/pricing))

**Five versions of Claude Code.** v2.1.202 through v2.1.206, Monday through Thursday night. The highlights: dynamic workflow size config and two new OpenTelemetry attributes in v2.1.202; a 30-plus-item reliability patch in v2.1.203 that fixed the macOS background session stall regression introduced six versions earlier (15 to 20 second freezes that users were diagnosing as their own network, not the tool); the Windows worktree deletion bug via NTFS junctions and silent message loss at the max-turns limit in v2.1.205; and in v2.1.206, a /doctor command that now proposes trimming oversized CLAUDE.md files before the token cost compounds silently. ([Source: GitHub](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/releases))

The pace is the product claim. Fifty-plus fixes across five versions in four days. The coding benchmark this week put Fable 5 at 77.2 on the Coding Agent Index, behind OpenAI's Sol at 80, using approximately twice the tokens per task. The lab's response to that gap: fix the floor, not the marketing.

---

## Quiet on the Wire

TypeScript SDK v0.111.0 shipped Friday afternoon with an API flag called "dreaming." The implementation: gates session tool calls on `evaluated_permission`, bounds idle behavior by `server stop_reason`. No public documentation shipped with it. This is the same sequencing Anthropic used for the advisor-tool beta and the agent-memory beta: SDK ships the implementation, documentation follows. "Dreaming" is the third undocumented capability in recent months. Watch the API release notes.

---

## The Close

Five code versions. A government scan. A reflective question on a Friday.

Anthropic spent this week fixing the floor and asking if the ceiling matters.

The dreaming flag is in the SDK. Nobody's explained it yet.
