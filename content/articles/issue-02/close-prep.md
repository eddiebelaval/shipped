# Shipped was last year's verb

A developer wakes at 6:12 AM to a Claude Code push notification. The agent fired the edit while they slept. Tests green. PR open. No one summoned it. The builder reads the diff, closes the laptop, refills the coffee. Day's already started.

Shipped was last year's verb. Present is this year's.

You were summoning Claude in March. In May you will be watching it watch. The products that arrive before you call for them are the ones you will trust by accident, and the trust surface they inherit is the one a tired admin clicked through six months ago. Your stack gets to ratify what the field chose this month, or explain why not.

## Draft scaffolding (below this line not for print)

### The Term-of-Issue landing

Presence should arrive mid-second-beat or in the third beat, never in a header. Reader earns the name.

Acceptable placements for the word "presence":
- In a clause of beat 2: "The product is not shipped. It is present."
- As the final word of beat 3: "Your stack becomes its presence."

Unacceptable placements:
- In a heading or subheading of the Close.
- As the first word of the Close.
- In the Close's opener beat.

## The cross-references

The Close ties back to specific moments in the issue. Pick the tie-backs that rhyme with the opener beat (see `front-of-book-draft.md`).

- If the Open anchors on Issue 01 shipping the same morning as Claude Design, the Close can rhyme with the time-stamp move.
- If the Open anchors on the WhatsApp reader signal, the Close can rhyme with the reader as actor.
- If the Open anchors on the "action layer not summary" meta beat, the Close can rhyme with the builder's agency.

Whichever Open beat is used, the Close rhymes. That is the STYLE.md Move C (rhythm closer).

## Voice notes for the prose

House moves for the Close (3-beat rhythm closer, per STYLE.md Move C):

- **Pattern 3 (stat-first jolt):** reserved for the Open, not the Close. Do not re-use.
- **Move C (rhythm closer):** three beats, last one bites. The structure is non-negotiable.
- **Punchline isolation:** the third beat's bite lands on its own line.
- **Sentence length:** varies. The final sentence is shorter than the two before it.
- **Second person:** allowed in beat 3 only. Anchor word: "your," "you."
- **"X isn't Y, it's Z" formula:** NOT allowed in the Close. Issue budget spent in the Lead. Hard kill.
- **Sass budget:** zero in the Close. Sass is earlier in the issue. The Close is dry with teeth.
- **Raoul Duke anchor:** single-detail characterization carries beat 1. Pick one concrete moment, not three.
- **Avoid the thesis restatement.** The Close is not a summary. Do not list the four threads. Let the reader carry them.

**Kill on sight** in the Close:

- *"to sum up"*
- *"in conclusion"*
- *"looking ahead"*
- *"one thing is clear"*
- *"only time will tell"*
- *"watch this space"*
- *"stay tuned"*
- *"the takeaway is"*
- *"what we learned this week"*
- *"the path forward"*
- *"the bottom line"*

## The stake

The call is: the Close is where the issue earns its Term. The tell is: where "presence" lands in the rhythm. What this means: three beats end with an inversion, not a summary. The posture is: the reader becomes subject in beat 3. The precedent is: Issue 00's "shadow release" Close, which named the pattern and let the reader carry it. The decision was made when the running order put Investigation before Quiet on the Wire; the Close inherits that arc. The bet is: the reader names "Presence" on their own by paragraph 2, and the Term block confirms what they already noticed. What Eddie chose when picking Pattern 3 (STYLE.md rhythm closer) is that the issue trusts the reader to finish the sentence.

## How this fits the issue

**Slot:** The Close. Mandatory core section. Word budget: 3 beats, approximately 120 to 200 words. Position: after the Term of Issue definition block (if run) or after Quiet on the Wire (if Term of Issue is absorbed into Close).

**Tie-backs required:**

**What the Close does NOT do:**

- Does not list the four arc threads.
