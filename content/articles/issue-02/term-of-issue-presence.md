# Presence

**Presence** /ˈprɛz.əns/ *noun*

The shift from AI-as-called-function to AI-as-ambient-condition. A product that absorbs context before a prompt arrives, responds to conditions rather than requests, and composes with adjacent surfaces by staying on instead of being invoked.

**First observable** 2026-04-17, the week Anthropic shipped Claude Design and staged Conway. OpenAI shipped Codex workspace agents the same week. Google shipped Gemini auto-browse in Chrome. Three labs converged on the pattern in seven days.

**Usage** *"We're not shipping a feature, we're extending our presence into the workspace."*

## For builders

Vetted moves, imperative, action-layer for naming and writing the Term block:

- Audit the definition against the four arc threads; Presence has to hold in all four without stretching.
- Rotate the usage example if it reads as abstract; Presence is named through a concrete verb, not a concept noun.
- Check the definition's verb; "absorb" and "watch" carry weight where "leverage" and "enable" do not.
- Verify the definition is narrow enough to fail falsification; a term that covers everything covers nothing.
- Test the block aloud; dictionary entries have rhythm too.
- Kill any hedge inside the block; definitions are declarative or they are footnotes.
- Watch for the "AI-first" temptation; Presence is not an AI phrase, it is an architectural one.
- Read the definition against Issue 00's "shadow release" naming act; measure whether this one earns the same weight.
- Monitor for the Term leaking into the Lead's prose; the Term should land only in the Term block and the Close.
- Test the definition against a builder who has not read the issue; if they ask "so what," the usage example is thin.

## The stake

The call is: Presence is the category the field is racing into. The tell is: Claude Design absorbs, Conway watches, Gemini enters Chrome, Codex enters the terminal. What this means: the next product layer is not invoked, it is ambient. The posture is: naming the category now lets builders design for it; naming it too late cedes the framing to the labs themselves. The precedent is: "cloud" earned its name in 2006, "serverless" in 2014, "agentic" in 2024. Presence is the 2026 candidate. The decision was made when three labs shipped variants of the pattern in the same week. The bet is: the term holds through six months of product cadence, and Issue 10 still uses it without footnote.

## Attribution caveats

- **"The shift from AI-as-called-function to AI-as-ambient-condition."** Editorial framing. Own it. Do not attribute to Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google.
- **"Presence-not-tools" corpus.** Eddie's essay in the voice corpus pre-dates this issue. Referenced internally; not quoted in the reader-facing definition.
- **"Ambient computing" precedent.** Mark Weiser, Xerox PARC, 1991. If cited, attribute correctly.
- **Usage examples involving specific products.** Each usage example must match what the product actually does (per the source article). Do not fabricate capabilities.

## How this fits the issue

**Slot:** Term of the Issue (optional shelf item per FORMULA). 100 to 150 word definition block. Position in the running order: after the Investigation, before the Close. The Close then rhymes with the term without restating it.

**Decision factor:** include only if the front-of-book has room after the Lead (200-300w), Conway (400-700w as B-story / Feature), Trust Week (400-700w Investigation), Also Shipped (3 items × 60-100w each), Quiet on the Wire (50-80w), and Companion choice (150-250w). A heavy issue (1,400w front-of-book) leaves about 80 to 150 words for the Term block. Fits.

**Alternative placement:** absorb into the Close. The Close's beat 2 can carry a compressed definition if the Term slot is skipped. Cost: the term is not explicitly named on its own page.

**Recommendation:** run as a standalone Term of the Issue block, with Close rhyming. The magazine's value is in the naming act; absorbing it into the Close hides the naming.

## Voice notes for the distilled prose

House moves for the 100 to 150 word definition block:

- **Pattern 2 (name-the-invisible-thing-then-define-it):** perfect match for the Term slot. Use it.
- **Move A (memoir to philosophy pivot):** NOT here. Too long a move for the word budget.
- **"X isn't Y, it's Z" formula:** NOT allowed. Issue budget spent in the Lead. Hard kill.
- **Raoul Duke anchor:** single-detail characterization in the usage example. Pick one product, one behavior.
- **Sass budget:** zero. The definition block is dry and clean. Sass lives elsewhere.
- **Second person:** NOT here. Reserve for the Close.
- **Tone:** dictionary-adjacent. Clinical, not editorial.

**Kill on sight** in the definition block:

- *"ushers in a new era of"*
- *"paradigm shift"*
- *"the next wave"*
- *"the agentic future"*
- *"redefines what it means to"*
- *"empowers users to"*
- *"AI-first"*
- *"human-in-the-loop"* (cliché, not useful here)
- *"post-chat"*
- *"beyond chatbots"*
